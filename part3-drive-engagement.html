How to Drive Engagement in Tactics

Research-backed levers for more likes, comments, shares, and clicks — and how to put them in the right order.

Four evidence-based pillars

🎬 Content & Format

  • Rich media (photo/video) beats plain text
  • Action-oriented posts (contests, challenges) drive participation
  • Emotional/transformational appeals outperform purely rational content
  • Content quality matters more than sheer volume
🧃 Coca-Cola's "Share a Coke" — emotional + participatory

📱 Platform, Timing & Frequency

  • TikTok & Instagram yield higher interaction rates than most other platforms
  • Weekday posts can outperform weekends on Facebook
  • Posting more ≠ more engagement unless quality stays high
  • Platform choice alone can lift engagement beyond changing content style
⚽ Football clubs posting match-day content on TikTok vs Facebook

💬 Interaction & Community

  • Replying to comments builds trust and grows engagement
  • Asking questions reliably boosts comment volume
  • Peer-to-peer discussion (Q&A, group moderators) deepens participation
  • Personalised responses > generic auto-replies
🏋️ FLEX Saigon replying to every comment on a "your first week" Reel

📊 Data-Driven Optimisation

  • Analytics & predictive models can significantly lift click-through rates
  • Test formats, topics, and timings continuously
  • Adjust content mix, platforms, and posting schedule based on performance data
📈 A/B testing two Instagram carousel versions for Volt Energy Drink

True or False? 🤔

Test your recall of the research findings. Click True or False for each statement.
Posting more often always leads to higher engagement, regardless of content quality.
❌ False — research shows higher frequency only helps when content quality stays high. Quality beats quantity every time.
Emotional and transformational content tends to generate more engagement than purely rational content.
✅ True — multiple studies (Drossos et al. 2023; Shahbaznezhad et al. 2021) confirm emotional/transformational appeals outperform rational-only content in engagement metrics.
Weekends consistently outperform weekdays for positive reactions on Facebook across all industries.
❌ False — research (Drossos et al. 2023) found weekday posts can actually outperform weekend posts for positive reactions on Facebook. Timing depends on your specific audience.
Switching to a more engaging platform (e.g., TikTok) can increase engagement more than changing content style alone.
✅ True — Shen (2023) found that for some brands, the platform choice itself was a bigger driver of engagement than content style adjustments.
Asking questions in posts reliably boosts the number of comments and conversations.
✅ True — confirmed by Moran et al. (2019), Sivaratnam et al. (2021), and Veale et al. (2015). Interactive prompts and questions are one of the most reliable engagement tactics.
